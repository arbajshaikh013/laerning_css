Ahmednagar's history centers on its founding by Ahmad Nizam Shah in 1494 as the capital of the Nizam Shahi dynasty (a Deccan Sultanate), a powerful medieval kingdom known for its fort, which later saw Mughal, Maratha, and British rule, including Jawaharlal Nehru's imprisonment during India's freedom struggle. The city, now officially Ahilyanagar, has a rich heritage from its origins on the site of ancient Bhingar, evolving from an independent sultanate to a significant center under various empires, notes Britannica.
