Aurangabad's history began in 1610 when Malik Ambar, prime minister of the Nizam Shahi dynasty, founded it as 'Khadki', making it his capital and a strategic Deccan center. Renamed Fateh Nagar, it later became Aurangabad in 1653 under Mughal Prince Aurangzeb (later Emperor Aurangzeb) when he became Deccan Viceroy, serving as his headquarters. The city is known for its Mughal and Sultanate heritage, including UNESCO sites like Ajanta & Ellora Caves, and landmarks like Bibi Ka Maqbara, reflecting centuries of Deccan
